Our Mission

We aim to bridge the gap between geospatial technology and mental health research, providing researchers, policymakers, and healthcare professionals with intuitive tools to visualize and analyze complex datasets.

By making this data accessible, we hope to contribute to better understanding and addressing the global mental health crisis.

About the Project

Project Background

This project investigates the effectiveness of two-dimensional (2D) and three-dimensional (3D) geospatial visualizations through a user study.

Traditional 2D maps often fail to capture the complexity of mental health data, which exists across multiple dimensions including time, demographics, and environmental factors.

Our platform addresses this by providing both 2D and 3D visualization tools, along with advanced statistical analysis capabilities.

Methodology

We aggregate data from multiple reliable sources including IHME, Our World in Data and peer-reviewed research studies. All data undergoes rigorous quality control and standardization before being incorporated into our system.

Our mapping techniques employ:

  • Geospatial analysis to visualize prevalence rates
  • Age-standardized prevalence rates
  • Time-series analysis for trends
  • Comparative visualization tools
